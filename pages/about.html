A bit about me

Making the abstract interactive.

I am a student at IIT Bombay who enjoys finding ways to translate the often invisible laws of physics into something tangible and easy to understand through code.

The Approach

Physics has always been my primary interest, but I found that programming is the best way to share that curiosity. I enjoy building simulations that turn complex phenomena into something my friends can play around with. When a problem doesn't have a clean analytical solution, I like using numerical methods to see how the system actually behaves.

Background & Projects

Academic Interests

Lately, I’ve been exploring computational physics at IIT Bombay, specifically looking into the electrical properties of quantum dots with the Maths and Physics Club. This interest in the fundamentals grew out of my time representing India at IPhO 2025, where I was fortunate enough to win a gold medal. For me, whether it's an Olympiad problem or a Desmos graph sent to a friend, the goal is always the same: understanding how things work by seeing them in motion.
